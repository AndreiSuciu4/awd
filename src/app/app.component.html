Definition

TEAM (noun)

A group of individuals who work together to achieve a common goal, with each member contributing their skills and responsibilities in a coordinated way.

TEAM (sports)

A group of athletes who compete together in a sport or game, aiming to achieve a better outcome than the opposing teams.

History

The word "team" comes from Old English, where the form "team" or "teama" was used to refer to a "team of horses" or a "draught cart," which was pulled by several horses. It originates from the Germanic word "*team" or "*tēman," which meant "to gather" or "to assemble," relating to the idea of a group of animals working together to perform a task.
By the 14th century, the word evolved to mean a group of people working together on a common project, and this meaning continued to expand into various fields. By the 16th century, "team" was also used to refer to groups of people collaborating in sports or professional activities.
Thus, "team" evolved from a concept tied to animals and physical labor to broader meanings, including groups of workers as well as teams in fields like sports, business, or science.

Perceptions

My mom: A team is a place where everyone helps and learns from each other to succeed together.

Context

In IT, the team is responsible for developing, implementing, and maintaining software or technological infrastructure. This can include programmers, UX/UI designers, system analysts, and network engineers. Their collaboration ensures the delivery of a functional and high-quality final product.

In a family, each member plays an important role, from parents who guide and protect, to children who learn and contribute to the family dynamic. The family team works together through mutual support, understanding, and collaboration to create a stable and harmonious environment.

In football, the team is responsible for collaborating among players to achieve the common goal of winning the game. Each player has a specific role (forward, defender, midfielder, goalkeeper), and success depends on how well they work together and follow the coach’s strategy.

In education, the team consists of teachers who guide and students who learn. The teacher is responsible for imparting knowledge and supporting the development of students, while students are responsible for learning and actively participating. Collaboration between the teacher and students helps create an effective educational environment.

In the military, the team is responsible for executing missions within a unit. Team members may include soldiers, officers, and other personnel who must work together to follow orders and protect national security. Communication, discipline, and cooperation are crucial in a military team.

In healthcare, the team consists of doctors, nurses, technicians, and other professionals who collaborate to provide care for patients. Each specialist has a specific role, and communication and teamwork are essential to deliver effective treatments and ensure the patient’s health

Word Forms

Teamwork: The collaboration among team members to achieve a common goal through communication and mutual support.

Teammate: It refers to a person who is part of the same team.

Teamship: The concept or spirit of a team, characterized by unity, cooperation, and mutual support within a group.
